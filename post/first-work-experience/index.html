First Work Experience

January 16, 2022

My first work experience was in the summer of 2005 when I Just turned 16 and finished the second year of high school (or was about to be junior in high school).
Early that summer my father said:
Go get a job son, and start working on your own
But he never told me how to.
I was fond of technical stuff since I was a child.
So the only search algorithm for a job that came to my mind was walking in the streets near where we lived and looking for electrical shops, facilities, technical services, motor winding stores, etc. to see whether there was anything available for me or not.

Despite my expectation, no one sneered at me or anything like that.
They even introduced me to their colleagues in order to find some other offers.
That’s how I got to be a shop assistant/ sales clerk at Avanes electric shop for almost three months.
My weekly salary was 0.3 $ and the job description there was:

  1. Repair cooler engine shell (which I learned by the end of the summer)
  2. Receive / Deliver turning orders
  3. Clean the shop and prepare tea at 10 o’clock every day.

These three months were one of the most influential periods in the formation of my personality although it may not seem to.
